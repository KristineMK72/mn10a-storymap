Bernie Penner for Minnesota House District 10A: A Story Map

Welcome to Bernie Penner's District 10A Story Map—a dynamic guide to the heart of central Minnesota's lake country and rural heartland, where community, opportunity, and resilience define our shared future. As a lifelong resident of central Minnesota, small business owner, and dedicated advocate for working families, Bernie Penner is running for the Minnesota House of Representatives in District 10A.

This district spans parts of Morrison, Crow Wing, Mille Lacs, Kanabec, and Aitkin Counties, including vibrant communities like Little Falls, Brainerd, Garrison, and Onamia, with a total population of about 43,200 (2025 estimates). It's known for its outdoorsy spirit, family farms, tourism, and resilient small-town economies.

This story map walks you through the district's demographics, economic drivers, key locations, and major issues facing our neighbors. Bernie's platform focuses on affordable housing, quality education, healthcare access, and sustainable economic growth—tailored to your priorities. Let's dive in.

1. The People of District 10A: Demographics at a Glance

District 10A embodies Minnesota's northwoods charm: Tight-knit communities along lakes and rivers, with a focus on family, faith, and the outdoors. Growth is steady but tied to seasonal tourism and remote work trends.

  • Population Breakdown (2025 Estimates): Total: 43,200 residents (up 1.5% from 2020). Age: 22% under 18; 60% working-age; 18% 65+. Diversity: 91% White; 3.5% American Indian; 2.1% Two or More Races; 1.8% Black; 1.2% Hispanic; 0.4% Asian.
  • Household Composition: 68% family households; Voter turnout: 76% in 2024.
  • Education Levels: 22% bachelor's or higher; 93% high school graduation.

Visual Snapshot: Picture snowmobile trails in winter, bass fishing on Mille Lacs in summer, and community bonfires—folks who value self-reliance and neighborly support.

Bernie supports expanding rural broadband, tribal partnerships, and trade apprenticeships to empower every resident.

2. Economic Drivers: Powering Prosperity

10A's economy thrives on nature's bounty and blue-collar grit: Lakes draw tourists, forests feed mills, and farms fuel the heartland.

  • Key Industries: Tourism (28%, $200M+ from Mille Lacs); Manufacturing (22%); Healthcare/Education (20%); Agriculture (15%).
  • Income & Affordability: Median Household: $62,800; Poverty: 11.2%; Unemployment: 4.1%; Median Home: $220,000.

Bernie's plan: Property tax relief for seasonal businesses, workforce grants for college grads staying local, and conservation easements to protect farmland.

3. Major Issues: Voices from the District

From bait shops in Garrison to diners in Little Falls, 10A folks prioritize practical fixes.

  • Healthcare Access: Rural shortages; 18-min ambulance times.
  • Education & Workforce: College expansions needed; youth outmigration.
  • Housing & Infrastructure: 1,200-unit shortage; flooding risks.
  • Environment: Lake protections; sustainable logging.
  • Economic Equity & Safety: Inflation impacts; volunteer fire strains.

Bernie commits to telehealth, school funding, and lake protection.

4. Key Locations: Where Community Happens

  • City Halls: Little Falls (301 1st St SE); Brainerd (222 S 6th St); Aitkin (201 Main St W); Onamia (35555 Round Lake Rd NW); Mille Lacs County (525 2nd St SE).
  • Other Spots: Central Lakes College; Mille Lacs Kathio State Park; Brainerd Lakes Airport.
